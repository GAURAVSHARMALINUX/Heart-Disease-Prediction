# Model Training and MLflow Tracking

## 1. Machine Learning Models
Two baseline classification algorithms were tested for this problem:
- **Logistic Regression**: A strong, interpretable baseline.
- **Random Forest**: An ensemble method that often captures non-linear relationships better without requiring extensive tuning.

## 2. Training Process
The training script (`src/train.py`) splits the data using `train_test_split` with stratification to ensure class proportions are maintained. Both models are trained, and their performances are evaluated on the test set. The model achieving the highest metric (Accuracy/F1-score) is persisted to `models/model.joblib`.

## 3. Experiment Tracking with MLflow
MLflow is deeply integrated into the training process to track every experiment run. The following information is logged automatically:
- **Parameters:** Model type, hyperparameter configurations.
- **Metrics:** Accuracy, Precision, Recall, F1-Score, and ROC-AUC.
- **Artifacts:** The trained Scikit-learn model object itself.

This approach guarantees full reproducibility and makes it easy to compare model iterations.
